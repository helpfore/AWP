BASIC ANTENNA CONCEPTS
CO1 — Definition, Patterns, Types, Radiation Mechanism
Definition & Functions

IEEE Std: "A means for radiating or receiving radio waves."

Circuit view: Impedance-matching device between feedline and free space (transitions Z₀ → η₀ = 377 Ω).

Transitional structure: Bridges guided-wave propagation (TL) to free-space radiation. Two-wire → flared → dipole → radiation.

Functions:

  • Radiate/receive EM energy efficiently
  • Provide directional selectivity (gain/pattern)
  • Match impedance of feedline to free space
  • Define polarisation of wave
Antenna vs Transmission Line

Radio Communication Link

  • Tx: Generates RF signal at frequency f
  • Tx Antenna: Converts guided wave → EM wave (gain G_T)
  • Free Space: Path loss ∝ (4πr/λ)² (FSPL)
  • Rx Antenna: Converts EM wave → guided wave (A_eff = G_R·λ²/4π)
P_R = P_T · G_T · G_R · (λ/4πr)² [Friis]

All Antenna Types

Wire Antennas

  • Short dipole: << λ; uniform current approx.; R_rad = 80π²(Δl/λ)² Ω
  • λ/2 dipole: Resonant; R_rad ≈ 73 Ω; D = 1.64 (2.15 dBi)
  • Monopole: λ/4 over ground; R_rad ≈ 36.5 Ω; D = 3.28 (5.15 dBi)
  • Loop: Small loop = magnetic dipole; large loop = resonant
  • Helix: Normal mode (compact) / Axial mode (CP, high-gain)
  • Yagi-Uda: Director + reflector array; G ≈ 7–15 dBi; terrestrial TV

Aperture & Other

  • Horn: Flared waveguide; microwave links, radar feed
  • Parabolic dish: Very high gain; satellite, radio telescope
  • Slot: Cut in conductor; flush-mount aircraft/ships
  • Microstrip patch: Low profile; GPS, mobile, Wi-Fi
  • Phased array: Electronic beam steering; 5G, military radar
  • Lens: Dielectric focusing; mm-wave systems

RADIATION OF ELECTRIC DIPOLE
CO3 — Field Derivations, Radiation Resistance, Reciprocity, Feeding Methods
Hertzian Dipole — All Field Components

Dipole IΔl at origin along z-axis, current I₀e^jωt:

A_z = μ₀I₀Δl·e^(-jβr)/(4πr)

H-field (only φ component):

H_φ = (I₀Δl sinθ/4π)·[jβ/r + 1/r²]·e^(-jβr)

E-field:

E_θ = ηI₀Δl sinθ/4π·[jβ/r + 1/r² - j/(β r³)]·e^(-jβr)
E_r = ηI₀Δl cosθ/2π·[1/r² + 1/(jβr³)]·e^(-jβr)

Far field (βr≫1), 1/r terms only:

E_θ = jηI₀Δlβ sinθ/4π · e^(-jβr)/r
H_φ = jI₀Δlβ sinθ/4π · e^(-jβr)/r
E_θ / H_φ = η = 120π ≈ 377 Ω
Half-Wave Dipole (λ/2)

Current distribution: I(z) = I₀cos(βz), |z|≤λ/4

Far-field components:

E_θ = j60I₀·(e^(-jβr)/r)·[cos(π/2·cosθ)/sinθ]
H_φ = E_θ/η

Pattern max at θ = 90° (broadside), nulls at θ = 0°,180°

Radiation Resistance:

P_rad = ½|I₀|²R_rad → R_rad = 2P_rad/|I₀|²
∫₀^π cos²(πcosθ/2)/sinθ dθ ≈ 1.2188
R_rad = 73.1 Ω ≈ 73 Ω (λ/2 dipole)

Monopole (λ/4): Image theory → half of dipole

R_rad(monopole) = 36.5 Ω; D = 3.28 (5.15 dBi)
Reciprocity Theorem

Statement: In a linear, bilateral network, if EMF V at port A causes current I at port B, then the same V at B causes same I at A. For antennas: radiation pattern, effective length, and impedance are identical in Tx & Rx modes.

Lorentz: ∫E_B·J_A dV = ∫E_A·J_B dV
∴ Z_12 = Z_21 → Pattern(Tx) = Pattern(Rx)

Consequence: Effective length h_e same in Tx & Rx; directional pattern same.

Antenna Feeding Methods
  • Coaxial probe: Direct feed; outer→gnd, inner→element
  • Microstrip edge: 50Ω line at edge (inset for matching)
  • Aperture (slot) coupling: Through slot in GND plane; good isolation
  • Inductive (loop): Coupling loop inside resonator
  • Proximity coupling: Overlap of feedline under patch; widest BW
  • Balun feed: For balanced antennas (dipole) from unbalanced coax
Retarded Effect & Field Regions

Retarded Potential: EM disturbance from source takes time r/c to reach P. Current at time t causes field at time t+r/c.

I_ret = I₀·e^(jω(t-r/c)) = I₀e^(jωt)·e^(-jβr)
A(r,t) = μ₀/4π ∫ J(r', t-R/c)/R dV'

Phase term e^(-jβr) = retardation factor

Radiation vs Induction field boundary:

E_rad ∝ 1/r; E_ind ∝ 1/r²
Equal when: βr = 1 → r = λ/2π

At 1 MHz: λ=300m → r = 300/2π ≈ 47.75 m

  • βr ≫ 1: Far field dominates
  • βr = 1: Transition point
  • βr ≪ 1: Reactive near field

ANTENNA PARAMETERS & DEFINITIONS
CO2 — Beam Area, Directivity, Gain, Aperture, Polarisation, Friis Formula, Temp & SNR
Beam & Pattern Parameters
HPBW
Angle between –3dB (half-power) points. Narrower = more directive.
FNBW
Angle between first nulls. FNBW ≈ 2×HPBW approx.
Beam Area Ω_A
Ω_A = ∫∫P_n dΩ = P_rad/U_max [sr]. Solid angle of equivalent uniform beam.
Beam Eff. η_B
η_B = Ω_MB/Ω_A where Ω_MB = main-beam solid angle. Fraction of power in main beam.
Rad. Intensity U
U = dP/dΩ [W/sr]. U = r²·W_rad. P_rad = ∫∫U dΩ
HPBW: U(θ) = U_max/2 at ±θ_hp
Ω_A = 4π/D (inversely proportional to D)
U₀(isotropic) = P_rad/4π [W/sr]
P_rad = ∫₀^2π∫₀^π U sinθ dθ dφ

Approx. gain from beamwidths:

G ≈ 41253 / (θ₁° × θ₂°) [unitless]

Approximate formula valid for pencil-beam antennas

Directivity, Gain & Efficiency

Directivity:

D = U_max/U_avg = 4πU_max/P_rad
D = 4π/Ω_A

Gain:

G = η_r × D where η_r = P_rad/P_in
G(dBi) = 10log₁₀(G)

Efficiency η_r accounts for ohmic + mismatch losses

Key values:

AntennaDdBi

Apertures & Effective Length

Effective Aperture A_e:

A_e = P_L / W_i [m²]
A_e = G·λ²/(4π) = D·λ²/(4π) (lossless)

Max Effective Aperture A_em:

A_em = η₀·L_eff²/(2R_rad) [derivation]

Physical aperture A_p: Actual cross-section area

Aperture efficiency:

η_ap = A_e / A_p (≤ 1, ideal = 1)

Effective (electric) length h_e:

V_oc = E_i · h_e → h_e = V_oc/E_i [m]

Equivalent uniform-current dipole producing same V_oc

  • Short dipole h_e = L/2
  • λ/2 dipole h_e = λ/π ≈ 0.318λ

Derivation result (key relation):

D = 4πA_em/λ² ↔ A_em = Dλ²/4π

Proved via Thevenin equivalent circuit of receiving antenna

Polarisation

Orientation of the E-field vector of the radiated wave.

Linear: E oscillates in one plane. Occurs when one E-component, or two in-phase components.

E = E_x x̂ cos(ωt-βz)

Circular (CP): Two equal-amplitude, 90° phase-shift components. AR = 1. RHCP (+), LHCP (−).

E = E₀[x̂cos(ωt-βz) ± ŷsin(ωt-βz)]

Elliptical: General case. AR = major/minor axis ratio.

TypeARCondition
Linear∞One component / in-phase
Circular1Equal amp, 90° phase
Elliptical1<AR<∞General case

Polarisation loss factor:

PLF = |ρ̂_w · ρ̂_a|² = cos²ψ

ψ = angle between wave & antenna polarisation vectors

PLF = 1 (matched), 0 (orthogonal = no reception)

Friis Transmission Formula & Link Budget

Derivation steps:

Step 1: W = P_T·G_T / (4πr²) [W/m²]
Step 2: A_eR = G_R·λ²/(4π)
Step 3: P_R = W·A_eR = P_T·G_T·G_R·(λ/4πr)²

Friis equation:

P_R/P_T = G_T · G_R · (λ/4πr)²

Free-Space Path Loss (FSPL):

FSPL = (4πr/λ)² = (4πrf/c)²
FSPL(dB) = 20log(4πr/λ)

Link Budget (dB form):

P_R = P_T + G_T + G_R − FSPL − L_misc

where L_misc = cable + polarisation + mismatch losses

Transmission Loss:

L = FSPL = 20log(4πr/λ) = 20log(f) + 20log(r) − 147.55

Loss ↑ with frequency (higher f → smaller λ → more FSPL) and with range r

Antenna Temperature & SNR

Antenna Temperature T_A: Effective noise temperature of the antenna due to thermal radiation from the environment it views (NOT physical temperature).

T_A = (1/4π) ∫∫ T_B(θ,φ)·P_n(θ,φ) dΩ [K]

T_B = brightness temperature of source in direction (θ,φ)

P_n = normalised power pattern

Noise power:

P_N = kT_sys·B [W] where k = 1.38×10⁻²³ J/K

T_sys = T_A + T_receiver

SNR = P_signal / P_noise = P_R / (kT_sys·B)

G/T ratio (figure of merit for receiving system):

G/T = G_R(dBi) − 10log(T_sys) [dB/K]

Higher G/T → better receive performance

LOOP ANTENNA
CO3 — Small Loop, Comparison with Dipole, Fields, Radiation Resistance, Directivity
Small Loop — Geometry & Principle

Loop of radius a (or area A = πa²), lying in x-y plane. Condition: circumference C = 2πa ≪ λ

Working principle: Oscillating current I₀ creates time-varying magnetic flux → time-varying magnetic dipole moment:

m = I₀·A = I₀·πa² [magnetic dipole moment]

This oscillating magnetic dipole radiates EM waves. Loop = magnetic dual of short electric dipole.

Far-field components:

E_φ = j(ηI₀πa²β² sinθ/4π)·e^(-jβr)/r
H_θ = −E_φ/η
All other components = 0 in far field

Pattern: sin θ — max at θ=90° (equatorial), null at θ=0° (axis)

Radiation Resistance & Directivity Proof

R_rad formula (small loop):

R_rad = 20π²(C/λ)⁴ = 20π²(2πa/λ)⁴ [Ω]
R_rad = 31,171·(A/λ²)² [Ω] (A = loop area)

Example: a = λ/25 → C/λ = 2π/25 = 0.2513

R_rad = 20π²×(0.2513)⁴ ≈ 0.788 Ω

Directivity proof (same as dipole):

U(θ) = U₀sin²θ [same pattern as dipole]
P_rad = ∫U dΩ = U₀·2π·∫₀^π sin³θ dθ = U₀·2π·4/3 = 8πU₀/3
D = 4πU_max/P_rad = 4πU₀/(8πU₀/3) = 3/2 = 1.5 ✓

Applications: AM radio (ferrite core), direction finding (sharp null), RFID/NFC, metal detectors, MRI coils, ELF submarine comms, EMC testing

Unrolled turn: right triangle with hyp=L, base=C, height=S, angle=α

Normal (Broadside) Mode

Condition: C ≪ λ and NA ≪ λ

Max radiation ⊥ to helix axis (broadside)

Pattern = figure-8 (same as dipole/loop)

Polarisation depends on α:

  • α → 0°: Loop-like → LP horizontal or CP
  • α → 90°: Dipole-like → LP vertical
  • Special α: Circular polarisation

CP condition:

S = C²/(2λ) [CP in normal mode]

Low gain; used in compact mobile handsets, hearing aids, pagers

Axial (End-Fire) Mode

Condition: C ≈ λ (0.75λ to 1.33λ)

Max radiation along helix axis (end-fire)

Always Circularly Polarised (RHCP or LHCP by winding)

Optimum pitch angle: α ≈ 12°–14°

G ≈ 11.8·N·(C/λ)²·(S/λ)
HPBW ≈ 52°·λ^(3/2)/(C·√NS) [deg]
R_in ≈ 140(C/λ) ≈ 140 Ω (at C=λ)
AR = (2N+1)/(2N) → 1 for large N

AR = 1 → perfect CP; AR→1 as N increases

Design & Wideband Characteristics

Design parameters (Axial mode):

  • C ≈ λ (0.75λ–1.33λ); D = C/π
  • S ≈ λ/4; pitch α ≈ 12–14°
  • N ≥ 3 turns (more turns → higher G)
  • GND plane diameter ≥ λ/2
  • Wire: 0.005λ ≤ d ≤ 0.05λ
  • Matching: Tapered 1st turn or λ/4 transformer to 50 Ω

Wideband characteristic:

Axial mode BW ≈ 1.7:1 (VSWR < 2). Wideband because helix geometry self-scales — as frequency varies, the active region shifts along the helix while maintaining C ≈ λ condition over a broad range.

BW ≈ 1.7:1 → unlike narrowband dipole (~1.05:1)

Pitch Angle & Axial Ratio — Definitions

Pitch Angle α: Angle between the helix tangent and a plane perpendicular to the helix axis.

tanα = S/C = S/(πD); 0° ≤ α ≤ 90°
  • α = 0° → flat loop (degenerate helix)
  • α = 90° → straight wire (degenerate helix)
  • α = 12–14° → optimal axial mode radiation

Axial Ratio (AR): Ratio of major axis (OA) to minor axis (OB) of polarisation ellipse.

AR = OA/OB = (2N+1)/(2N) [for helix]
  • AR = 1 → perfect RHCP or LHCP
  • AR = ∞ → linear polarisation
  • 1 < AR < ∞ → elliptical polarisation

Ground Wave Propagation

Wave travels along the surface of the Earth, inducing currents in the surface. Power is continuously absorbed.

  • Requires vertical polarisation (horizontal gets short-circuited by surface)
  • Attenuation increases with frequency (∝ f²)
  • Range higher over seawater (σ = 4 S/m) than dry land (σ = 0.001 S/m)
  • Works below 3 MHz; at higher f, too much attenuation

Attenuation factor:

A ≈ exp(−α·d) where α ↑ with f and ↑ with low σ_ground
Ionosphere Structure & Layers

Region 60–1000 km; ionised by solar UV & X-rays. Free electrons refract radio waves.

LayerHeightCharacteristic
D60–90 kmDaytime only; absorbs HF/MF, causes attenuation
E100–125 kmDay; partial reflection; Sporadic-E possible
E_s~110 kmSporadic; unpredictable; strong HF/VHF reflection
F₁170–220 kmDaytime only; merges with F₂ at night
F₂250–400 kmMost important; exists day & night; long-range HF

Electron density N_e:

n = √(1 − 81N_e/f²) [refractive index]

When n < 1: wave bends away from vertical

When n = 0: total reflection occurs

Critical frequency f_c: Maximum frequency for vertical incidence reflection from a layer

f_c = 9√N_max [Hz] where N_max in e/m³

Typical f_c(F₂): 5–15 MHz (day), 3–6 MHz (night)

Troposphere Structure

Region 0–12 km above Earth. Space waves travel here.

Normal refraction: n decreases with altitude → wave bends slightly downward, extending LOS range beyond geometric horizon.

dN/dh ≈ −40 N-units/km (standard atmosphere)

Effective Earth radius:

r_e = k·r₀ = (4/3)×6371 km (k = 4/3 for std atm)

Radio horizon:

d = √(2r_e·h_T) + √(2r_e·h_R) [m]

where h_T, h_R = Tx/Rx antenna heights

Sky Wave — Key Definitions
Virtual Height h'
Apparent height of reflection if wave traveled at speed of light. h' > actual layer height h.
Skip Distance d_s
Minimum ground distance covered by sky wave for given f. No coverage between TX and skip distance (dead zone).
Dead Zone
Region between ground wave limit and skip distance. No reception.
MUF
Maximum Usable Frequency — max f that returns to Earth via ionosphere at a given angle of incidence.
OWF
Optimum Working Frequency = 0.85 × MUF. Chosen for reliable communication (avoids MUF margin).
LUF
Lowest Usable Frequency — min f above which D-layer absorption is tolerable.
Multi-hop
Wave reflected multiple times between ionosphere and Earth to reach very long distances (global comms).
MUF = f_c / sinΦ = f_c · sec i [sec law]
d_skip = 2h'·√((MUF/f)²−1) [approx]
OWF = 0.85 × MUF
Fading & Ionospheric Absorption

Fading: Time variation in received signal strength due to:

  • Multi-path fading: Multiple sky-wave paths arrive with different phases → constructive/destructive interference
  • Absorption variation: D-layer absorption changes with solar activity
  • Polarisation fading: Faraday rotation of polarisation in ionosphere
  • Selective fading: Different frequencies fade differently

Ionospheric Absorption: D-layer electrons collide with neutral atoms, converting wave energy to heat. Increases with f⁻². Worst at low HF (3–5 MHz). Increases during solar flares (SID — Sudden Ionospheric Disturbance).

Absorption A ∝ N_e·ν/(f ± f_H)² [non-deviative]
Space Wave & Super Refraction

Space Wave: Combination of direct LOS wave + ground-reflected wave. Used at VHF and above.

E_total = E_direct + E_reflected (with phase shift)

Ground reflected wave has phase shift (π for smooth surface). Interference → lobing pattern in elevation.

Super Refraction (Ducting): Abnormal tropospheric condition where temperature inversion or rapid decrease in humidity creates a layer with strong dN/dh << −40 N-units/km. The wave is bent so sharply it is trapped in a "duct" between ground and the inversion layer.

  • Allows VHF/UHF propagation far beyond normal LOS
  • Can cause interference between distant stations
  • Common over warm seas (evaporation duct)
  • Duct height: 10–100 m (surface duct) to 1–3 km (elevated duct)
Super refraction: dN/dh < −157 N-units/km
